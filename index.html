My Timeline

What have I been up to until now?

  • 2005 2006

    Varsity College

    Although a very basic introduction to the developer life, it confirmed my suspicions that to become a developer would be fun and challenging! At the same time, I gained the required knowledge to further strengthen my developing skills.

  • 2007 February May

    Clabo Beleggings

    Being the IT and technical support at Clabo presented its share of challenges, broad challenges. From Dovecot mail servers, VPN maintenance, networking installations and even technical support. Only one thing was missing, me sitting in front of a computer and doing actual coding, so I moved on, in search of more coding!

  • 2008 February July

    Van Zyl & Pritchard

    Studying at VZap was quite an experience. It was an intensive course, running from 8am to 5pm everyday and slapped all of us with a steep learning curve. Hard work payed off and so during that time I walked away with a fair grasp of Object Orientated Programming. It was intriguing to experience open source technologies firsthand. So time passed quickly and now deemed myself ready to become a developer!

  • 2008 August October

    Cbars

    Fresh out of college (again) and ready to become a developer, I started work at Cbars, getting my hands on proper web development for the first time. Here I changed back again from open source to Microsoft, but soon found myself regretting the decision. Due to my background in ASP classic, I was tasked with optimizing pages upon pages of code written by another gentleman. All the new and fresh web developing technologies were flying past me while stuck with ASP classic.

  • 2008 November 2016 February

    Manstrat AIS

    So Manstrat was my chance to show what I am capable of. At the beginning I was spending most of my time developing in VB.Net, ASP.Net and quickly progressed to using ajax. Next came c#, jquery, handlebars. Bringing with them plenty of happy developing hours. A few years flew past and when I looked back, I noticed that I was trending more and more towards letting JavaScript based technologies do the work for me. Not forgetting that I was still actively and fiercely busy developing the same on the backend. Constantly improving the technologies. For our latest project we decided to employ AngularJs, ASP.Net Web API's, Entity Framework 6 and MS SQL 2014 and I must say, what a lovely stack!

  • 2016 February Present

    Britehouse (Standard Bank SBG)

    I started at Britehouse in search for better opportunities and possibly the chance of moving up and reaching my dream of becoming a software architect. At the moment I am being outsourced to Stadard Bank as a front-end developer. Here I am able to dev web applications using Angular 2 (typescript) with Material Design Lite to create some rich and interactive user interfaces. Ever heard of NgRx? Or maybe Redux or flux? Yup, we are using it...quite cool. Getting use to the Agile environment and enjoying the free coffee!

    Who Knows?
