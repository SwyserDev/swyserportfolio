My Timeline

What have I been up to until now?

  • 2005 2006

    Varsity College

    Although a very basic introduction to the developer world, it confirmed my suspicions that to become a developer would be fun and challenging! At the same time I gained the required knowledge to establish a good foundation on which I was able to further strengthen my software development skills.

  • 2007 February May

    Clabo Beleggings

    Being in the postition of overseeing IT and technical support at Clabo presented its share of challenges, broad challenges. From Dovecot mail servers, VPN setup and maintenance, networking installations and even technical support. The only thing that I was missing, me sitting in front of a computer and doing actual coding, so I moved on, in search of more coding challenges!

  • 2008 February July

    Van Zyl & Pritchard

    Studying at VZap was quite an experience. It was an intensive course running from 8am to 5pm everyday and slapped all of us with a steep learning curve. Not being afraid of hard work payed off and so during that time I walked away with a SOLID grasp of Object Orientated Programming. It was intriguing to experience open source technologies firsthand. So time passed quickly and now deemed myself ready to become a proper software developer!

  • 2008 August October

    Cbars

    Fresh out of college (again) and ready to become a developer, I started work at Cbars, getting my hands on actual web development for the first time. At Cbars I changed back again from open source to Microsoft, but soon found myself regretting the decision. Due to my background in ASP classic, I was tasked with optimizing pages upon pages of code written by another gentleman. All the new and fresh web developing technologies were flying past me while stuck with ASP classic.

  • 2008 November 2016 February

    Manstrat AIS

    So Manstrat was my chance to show what I am capable of. At the beginning I was spending most of my time developing in VB.Net, ASP.Net and quickly progressed to using ajax. Next came c#, jquery, handlebars seeing that the powers of Microsft and opensource JavaScript libraries can indeed compliment each other, bringing with them plenty of happy developing hours. A few years flew past and when I looked back, I noticed that I was trending more and more towards letting JavaScript based technologies do the work for me. Not forgetting that I was still actively and fiercely busy developing the same on the backend using WebApi's. I found myself constantly improving the technology stack the we used. For our latest project we decided to employ AngularJs, ASP.Net Web API's, Entity Framework 6 and MS SQL 2014 and I must say, what a lovely stack it was! It was then that I found out that DevOps is a very crutial part of any project!

  • 2016 February Present

    Britehouse (Standard Bank SBG)

    I started at Britehouse in search for better opportunities and possibly the chance of moving up and reaching my dream of becoming a software architect. At the moment, I am being outsourced to Standard Bank as a front-end developer. Here I am able to dev web applications using Angular 2 (typescript) with Material Design Lite to create some rich and interactive user interfaces. Ever heard of RxJS? Yup, we are using it...quite cool. I now can't imagine a project without using the Agile principles to manage it all. I retrospect I have grown more in the last year than I have grown at any other company. Being exposed to other developers opinions, having a passionate mentor and being able confirm my after-hours studies the colleagues is truly valuable!
